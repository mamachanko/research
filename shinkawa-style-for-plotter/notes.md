## Key Findings

### Materials
- Primary tool: Pentel Brush Pen, specifically XFL2V (Sukiho) model
- Also uses felt-tip pens, pencils, correction pen (white-out)
- Paper: regular A4 copy paper (not fancy stock)
- Digital: Photoshop for adjustments, Corel Painter for digital line work
- Toolkit self-described: "A4 copy paper, pencil, eraser, brush pen, correction pen, and Photoshop"

### Process
1. Many rough doodle-like sketches first
2. Draws on paper with pencil or brush pen
3. Scans and adjusts in Photoshop
4. Kojima quote: "extremely fast at drawing silhouettes" — grasps essence first, then adds outline to energetic brushstrokes, fills details

### Distinctive Technique: Layered Ink + Correction Fluid
- Paints on copy paper repeatedly with thin black ink AND correction ink
- Paper becomes "crispy" from layering
- Underlying material shows through like oil paint
- This creates the rough, textured, deconstructed look

### Line Characteristics
- Few straight lines — everything curves and flows
- Extreme line weight variation from brush pressure
- Strokes appear to "burst onto the page in a few quick movements"
- Confident single strokes rather than built-up lines
- Goes "straight into shading" rather than using traditional outlines

### Tonal Approach
- Diluted ink washes NOT for accurate shading but for "compositional impact"
- High contrast: deep blacks against white paper
- Correction fluid adds white highlights on top of ink
- Three-value system: black ink, diluted gray wash, white paper/correction fluid

### Composition
- Heavy use of negative space — large blank areas
- "Drawn in negative" — empty space is active design element
- Close-up portraits and dynamic poses
- Weight distribution always evident (gravity feels real)
- Silhouette-first thinking

### Influences Relevant to Understanding Style
- Yoshitaka Amano (greatest influence — brush work specifically)
- Frank Miller (high contrast black/white)
- Aubrey Beardsley (graphic quality, negative space)
- Sumi-e tradition (ink wash, calligraphic strokes)
- Moebius (line quality, detail)

## Pen Plotter Translation Challenges
- No existing pen plotter projects found attempting this style
- Variable brush width is the #1 challenge — plotters use fixed-width pens
- Ink wash gradients require different approach (hatching? multiple passes?)
- Spontaneous energy of brushwork vs. mechanical precision
- Correction fluid layering has no direct plotter equivalent
- Could potentially use: variable-speed plotting, multiple pen widths, diluted ink pens, overlapping strokes for wash effects
